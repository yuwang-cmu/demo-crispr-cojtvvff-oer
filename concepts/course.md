# Course Concept Map — CRISPR and Genome Editing

> **Demonstration only:** this AI-generated Coursewerk package is not intended for direct use in actual teaching.

## Organizing question

How does a microbial sequence-recognition system become an engineered intervention, and what evidence is required to distinguish a molecular event from a justified biological claim?

## Summary

The course follows a causal chain from phage pressure through spacer acquisition, CRISPR memory, guide formation, PAM-dependent recognition, and Cas action. Engineering adds delivery and a programmed molecular event; cellular repair then creates allele distributions. Controls and orthogonal assays support the later transitions from genotype through function and phenotype to a bounded causal claim.

## Concept sequence

1. Phage pressure leads to spacer acquisition and a sequence record in a CRISPR array.
2. Array expression and RNA processing produce guide-bearing effector complexes.
3. PAM recognition, guide pairing, and conformational checkpoints govern Cas action.
4. Engineered targeting and delivery create a molecular exposure, not a guaranteed edit.
5. Editing chemistry and cellular repair produce an allele distribution.
6. Molecular assays, functional evidence, controls, and orthogonal tests support a bounded interpretation.

## Chapter sequence

1. **CRISPR as a Biological System** explains acquisition, expression, interference, Cas9 targeting, and why natural diversity matters.
2. **Editing Genomes** reframes editing as a workflow involving target choice, delivery, repair, validation, uncertainty, applications, and governance.

## Cross-chapter threshold concepts

- CRISPR is a family of natural defense systems; CRISPR–Cas9 is one engineered implementation.
- A spacer or guide encodes sequence preference, while PAM recognition, molecular conformation, and cellular context affect action.
- The programmable component creates an opportunity for change; endogenous repair processes produce many final genotypes.
- Efficiency, specificity, and biological effect are different quantities measured with different assays.
- Somatic treatment, heritable editing, research models, agriculture, and ecological interventions have different benefit, risk, consent, and governance structures.
